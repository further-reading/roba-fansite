Combat is a core part of ROBA, taking place during the Action Phase. The order in which combat steps are taken is important as breaking the flow can give your opponent an edge.

Combat Steps

  1. Declaration Phase

    1. Attacker declares target and participants of attack.
    2. Defender declares if blocking.

  2. Clash Phase

    1. Attacker clashes.
    2. Defender clashes.
    3. Clash cards revealed.
    4. Clash card effects applied.

  3. Resolution Phase

    1. Damage and shield tallied.
    2. Defender declares target
    3. Damage applied
    4. Power added

Declaration Phase

  1. Attacker declares target and participants of attack.
  2. Defender declares if blocking.

All attacks start with the Declaration Phase. This phase begins by the attacker declaring the target for their attack. they can also make it a team attack by involving multiple characters. The attacker should commit the participants to make it clear to the defender who is involved. The target can be an opposing conduit or hero in the same lane as the attacking hero(s).

If the defender has another ready hero in the lane, they may declare a block. The block will change the target of the attack, however as a cost the blocking hero's base damage is reduced to 0 and will become committed.

Due to the cost of blocking, there are a lot of factors to consider when choosing the target and whether to block.

Clash Phase

There is no clash step when the target is a conduit. If the defender is not blocking then move straight to the resolution phase

  1. Attacker clashes.
  2. Defender clashes.
  3. Clash cards revealed.
  4. Clash card effects applied.

Once the participants have been declared the clash phase can begin. Clashing means to play a card face down, but remember that clashing is optional - you may choose to play no card.

When you are clashing with multiple heroes you must also declare which hero is clashing. Any clash effects, shields, etc will be applied to just this hero.

After the Defender plays step the card are revealed and any clash effects are applied. Clash effects are written at the bottom of each card and have the Clash keyword.

If a card says you can clash again immediately it means you can immediately play another card in this combat. Unless the card states otherwise, you will then apply any clash effects and apply its damage and shield values in the next step.

Resolution Phase

  1. Damage and Shield amounts tallied.
  2. Defender declares target.
  3. Damage applied.
  4. Power added.

In the resolution phase first tally the total damage done by participants in the combat. You do this by adding up the attack values on your cards. If this was a team attack, the defender can then choose who will take damage from their hero, unless one of the heroes has Vanguard.

Remember, back in the clash phase the attacker had to choose who plays the card - only that hero gets the shield bonus from the card.

Once the total damage is tallied, it is applied to the hero, less any shield rating the hero has. Damage occurs simultaneously, so any defeated hero will still apply their full damage. Killing a hero or conduit will generate power for the opposing player. A hero is worth 2 points and a conduit is worth 1 point.

Note that cards like Mato's Battle Frenzy won't get bonuses for being damaged since the damage was not present during the clash phase.

If two Deities are defeated in the same combat, the attacker wins the game.

Example of Play

Declaration Phase

  1. The attacker declares that he will attack the defender's conduit with Mato and Corvus, committing them.
  2. The defender declares that she will block with Octavian, which commits him.

Clash Phase

  1. The attacker clashes by placing a card phase down next to Corvus.
  2. The defender clashes by playing a card face down next to Octavian.
  3. The attacker reveals that he has played Battle Frenzy while the defender reveals that she has played Eternal Discipline.
  4. The attacker resolves his clash effect for Battle Frenzy by adding 1 attack to Corvus for every 2 wounds on him.
  5. The defender resolves his clash effect for Eternal Discipline by clashing again immediately. She chooses to play 10,000 drills and chooses to add its +2 bonus to attack.

Resolution Phase

  1. The attacker adds together the attack scores of Mato, Corvus and Battle Frenzy together, including any extra attack Corvus may have gained from Battle Frenzy phase.
  2. The attacker adds together the shield scores that Mato and Corvus have separately.
  3. The defender adds together the attack and shield scores of Octavian, Eternal Discipline and 10,000 drills. Note that because Octavian is blocking his attack is 0, but he will still gain attack scores from clash cards.
  4. The defender chooses which of the two attacking heroes to apply her damage to.
  5. The targets' shield scores are subtracted from the incoming damage to determine how many wounds each side takes.
  6. Any knocked out heroes are removed and the combat ends.
